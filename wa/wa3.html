Intro

A film genre is a category of movies that share similar stories, styles, or emotions.

Genres

The 14 main genres of film are Action, Adventure, Animated, Comedy, Drama, Fantasy, History, Horror, Musical, Romance, Sci Fi, thriller,Western, Hybrid

Sub Genres

Subgenres mix two different genres. Genres can also blend with unrelated ones to form hybrid genres, like romantic comedies and action comedies.

History

Since the 19th century, the term "genre" has been used to categorize films by type, evolving in significance with debates like auteur theory versus genre in the 1950s, and becoming a key part of film theory by the late 1960s.

Early Hollywood favored genre films for their marketability and ease of production, with clear conventions allowing for an assembly-line approach, exemplified by James Bond films with their consistent formula of action, gadgets, and colorful villains.

Audience

Genres have built-in audiences and publications, aiding in marketing, criticism, and consumption analysis, while screenwriters use genres to craft plots, though often lacking originality unless they transcend traditional forms.
